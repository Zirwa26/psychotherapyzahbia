About
Hi, I’m Zahbia Sarfraz MA.

Lahore based psychotherapist and a registered member of the British Association for Counseling and Psychotherapy.
" I meet people
where they are, and
walk alongside
them to where they want to be. "
I offer a relational and integrative approach to counseling drawing on principles from various psychotherapeutic approaches rooted in psychodynamic and humanistic traditions. The foundation of my approach is based on a relationship of compassion, trust and respect. At different times in our lives we may find ourselves losing direction or searching for meaning. The key to my work is to collaboratively work with my clients to notice unconscious dynamics and patterns. I view the body and mind as a holistic system in which the psyche expresses itself through thought processes, emotions, sensations, imagery, and relational dynamics. I have a particular interest in dreams, spirituality and life-threatening illnesses. I’m particularly interested in how a crisis also offers the potential for a transformation.

Clinical Experience

I have experience working with adult individuals, in short and long-term psychotherapy, couples, families. I have worked in private setting and agency setting which has given me the tools to provide therapy for a diverse range of clients. I have taught at various institutes in Lahore including Lahore University of Management Sciences (LUMS) and the Lahore School of Economics (LSE). Along with this, i have set up the guidance counseling system for the central region of The City School network which consists of 44 schools. Additionally, I have been involved in projects with K-Electric, British Council and Shirkat Gah.
